Why this exists

This started as a feeling, not an idea.

I've always felt invisible. Not in the way people say when they want attention — in the quieter way. Like the world moves around me while I'm standing just outside of it.

I'm Amira, a Unity developer from Egypt. For four years, I built real-time physics simulations for students — virtual biology, chemistry, and physics labs. It was meaningful work, and I learned a lot from it. But after a while, I realized something was missing.

At some point, I realized how long it had been since I had created something that truly felt like my own.

I chose this career because I loved making games. But somewhere between projects, deadlines, and responsibilities, I had slowly drifted away from the passion that brought me here in the first place.

That realization stayed with me for a long time.

So in 2026, I decided to return to where it all began: making games. When I started thinking about my first personal project, I knew I wanted it to be made from the things that had stayed with me throughout my life. Part of it came from my work and my love for science — especially physics, where simple rules can create surprisingly beautiful outcomes. Part of it came from the things I was obsessed with as a teenager: exploration, mysteries, abandoned places, and the feeling of being alone with your thoughts. And part of it came from my love of ravens. They're intelligent, curious, and deeply fascinating creatures. The more I learned about them, the more I felt they belonged in this world.

"A lonely girl. A silent raven. An abandoned alchemist's house."

At first, Nox was just a character. But the more I worked on her, the more I realized she was carrying some of the same questions I carried myself
I didn't plan that.
It just happened.

This journal exists to document the journey of building this game — the decisions, the failures, the discoveries, and the moments when everything suddenly clicks.
Not the finished story.
Just the real one.

Characters

Nox — you

Age 10–12. Dark short hair. Honey amber eyes. Dark striped blouse, short skirt, black tights, worn boots. The player never sees her — just as the world never truly saw her. She exists only through her hands reaching out, and the raven who looks back.

The Raven — silent, nameless

He belonged to the alchemist. He was there when everything happened. He never speaks. He communicates through where he sits, what he drops, and when he looks away. His story is not about guiding Nox. It is about learning to let go.

The Alchemist — never seen

Gone before the story begins. Present in every room. He felt the same curse. He spent his whole life trying to formula his way out of it. The house is his mind. Every room, a chapter of his search.
